What can be infered from the data

  • Workers in poorer countries tend to work more hours on average than their counterparts in wealthier countries. This has had considerable implications on economic progress over the last century, and the nature of inequality between countries today. This equates to citizens of countries like Cambodia are not only financially poor but also leisure poor, as their economies have low productivity and require longer hours. Longer hours worked decreases spare time to invest in improving their conditions through education. The data shows the difference in prosperity is not due to work ethic, but due to differences in circumstance and opportunity.
